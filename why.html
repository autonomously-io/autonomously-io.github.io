Why Autonomously?

The Problem

Modern networks generate massive volumes of signals — logs, metrics, traces, alerts, topology changes — across layers and vendors. Operations teams are drowning in data, yet still lack understanding.

Existing tools observe. They collect, they visualize, they alert. But they leave the hardest question to humans: "Why is this happening?"

Beyond Observability

Observability tells you what is happening. We believe the next step is Understandability — correlating multi-source signals to reveal why it is happening. Not just dashboards and alerts, but causal reasoning and root cause analysis.

Two Pillars

Understandability

Fuse signals across sources, layers, and vendors to uncover causal relationships and identify root causes.

Closed Loop Automation

Don't stop at understanding. Detect, understand, act, and verify — closing the loop autonomously.

Observe → Correlate → Understand → Act → Verify → ⟲

Why "Autonomously"

Not "automated." Not "assisted." Autonomous.

Automation follows scripts. Autonomy means the system understands context, reasons about causality, and decides how to restore order — then verifies the outcome. The goal is a network that heals itself, not one that pages a human faster.

Bridging Telecom and IT

Telecom has decades of standards for autonomous operation — TMF, ETSI ZSM, intent-based networking. IT has cloud-native observability, AIOps, and SRE practices. These worlds have evolved in parallel. a10y bridges them.
